FAQs

What is OGG Vorbis format?

OGG is a container format, and Vorbis is the audio codec inside it. Together, OGG Vorbis is a completely free, open-source audio compression format with no patents or licensing fees. It achieves better compression than MP3 at similar quality levels.

Is OGG conversion free?

Yes, Y2mate offers completely free YouTube to OGG conversion. Download unlimited OGG Vorbis files with no subscription, no hidden costs, and no file size limits. The OGG format itself is also patent-free for commercial use.

OGG vs MP3—which is better?

OGG Vorbis achieves better audio quality than MP3 at the same bitrate, or equal quality at 15-20% smaller file size. OGG is patent-free and open source. MP3 has wider device support but uses licensed technology. For web/gaming, OGG is superior.

Why isn't OGG supported on all devices?

Apple devices (iPhone, iPad, Mac) historically excluded OGG to promote their own AAC format. Modern web browsers support OGG natively. For maximum compatibility, convert OGG to MP3 for playback on Apple devices.

Can I use OGG in my game or app?

Yes! OGG is completely patent-free and royalty-free. Unity, Unreal Engine, Godot, and HTML5 games all support OGG natively. No licensing fees ever—perfect for commercial projects and indie developers.

What bitrate should I use for OGG?

For music, 160-192kbps OGG equals 192-256kbps MP3 quality. For voice/podcasts, 96-128kbps OGG is sufficient. OGG's superior compression means you can use lower bitrates without sacrificing perceived audio quality.

Does OGG support metadata and tags?

Yes, OGG Vorbis supports Vorbis Comments for metadata—artist, album, track number, artwork, and more. Tagging works similarly to MP3 ID3 tags and is supported by most music players and library managers.
