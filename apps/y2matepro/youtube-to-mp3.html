Frequently Asked Questions

How does MP3 bitrate affect audio quality?

Bitrate defines how much audio data is allocated per second during compression. Higher bitrates preserve more frequency detail and reduce compression artifacts, especially in complex music with wide dynamic range. Lower bitrates reduce file size but may introduce perceptual loss in high-frequency content.

When should I choose 128kbps, 192kbps, or 320kbps?

128kbps is suitable for speech-based content such as lectures or podcasts. 192kbps provides a balance between file size and quality for everyday listening. 320kbps is recommended for music with rich instrumentation or when using high-quality headphones or speakers.

Does the MP3 file include the full audio from the video?
