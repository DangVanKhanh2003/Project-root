Why Choose FLAC Format?

  • Lossless compression: No quality loss – audio is bit-for-bit identical to the original source.
  • Efficient storage: 40-50% smaller than uncompressed WAV files.
  • Hi-Res audio support: Up to 32-bit depth and 384kHz sample rate for studio-quality audio.
  • Open & free: Royalty-free, open-source format with no patent restrictions.
  • Wide compatibility: Natively supported on Windows 10, macOS, iOS, and Android.
  • Perfect for archiving: Future-proof format that preserves audio exactly as recorded.
  • Rich metadata: Supports album art, tags, and cue sheets via Vorbis comments.

What does "lossless" mean in FLAC?

Lossless means the audio is compressed without losing any data. When decoded, it's bit-for-bit identical to the original.

Is FLAC better than MP3?

Yes, for quality. FLAC preserves 100% of the original audio while MP3 discards data. However, FLAC files are larger than MP3.

What's the difference between 16-bit and 24-bit FLAC?

24-bit offers higher dynamic range (144dB vs 96dB) and is used for Hi-Res audio. 16-bit/44.1kHz is CD quality and sufficient for most listeners.

Can I play FLAC on my device?

Yes. FLAC is natively supported on Windows 10, macOS, iOS, Android, and most modern audio players.

How much smaller is FLAC than WAV?

FLAC typically reduces file size by 40-50% compared to uncompressed WAV while maintaining identical audio quality.

Does YTMP3 store my data?

No. All conversions are temporary, and no personal data is stored.

Is FLAC good for music archiving?

Yes. FLAC is ideal for archiving because it's lossless, open-source, and can be converted to any format without generation loss.
